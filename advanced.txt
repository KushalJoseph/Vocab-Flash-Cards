unforthcoming - one meaning of forthcoming is “willing to share easily”, hence this means “uncooperative”, not willing to give up information
The teacher demanded to know who broke the window while she was out of the room, but the students understandably were unforthcoming

unimpeachable - “impeach” means to challenge or question the credibility or integrity. So this means trustworthy, reliable, and free from any accusation or criticism
After his long and unimpeachable service to the company, Sharat felt that a gold watch was a slap in the face rather than an honor

unprepossessing - unfavorable or neutral first impression, usually a lack of charm, elegance, or visual appeal
World leaders coming to meet Gandhi would expect a towering sage, and often would be surprised by the unprepossessing little man dressed only in a loincloth and shawl

unpropitious - little chance of success, not favorable or propitious
With only a bottle of water and a sandwich, the hikers faced an unpropitious task: ascending a huge mountain that took most two days to climb

unstinting - "stint," means to limit, restrict, or be sparing in providing, hence this means very generous, not just for money, may be used for time
Helen is unstinting with her time, often spending hours at the house of a sick friend

untenable - cannot be defended or justified (referring to a theory)
After Galelio and Kepler’s theories, the old geocentric theory became untenable to most of the educated people in Europe

untoward - inconvenient, unfavorable
Some professors find teaching untoward (inconvenient) as having to prepare for lectures and conduct office hours prevents them from focusing on their research

untrammeled - Not restricted, hindered, or limited by rules, obstacles, or constraints. It suggests a sense of freedom, independence
The young couple embarked on an untrammeled adventure, leaving behind their jobs and responsibilities to travel the world and embrace the joy of exploration.

unviable - not viable, i.e, not able to succeed or work, also “inviable”
The plan was obviously unviable considering that it lead to complete environmental destruction in the river valley.

vaunted - highly praised and boasted about
For years, they had heard of New York City's vaunted skyline, and when they finally saw it, it did not disappoint

venial - (not to be confused with “venal”), not a big crime, easily excused or forgiven; pardonable
His traffic violations ran the gamut from the venial to the egregious--on one occasion he simply did not come to a complete stop; another time he tried to escape across state lines at speeds in excess of 140 mph

ran the gamut - cover a wide range or variety of something, full extent of the spectrum
The speaker's presentation ran the gamut of emotions, taking the audience from tears of laughter to moments of deep reflection.

verisimilitude - the appearance of truth or being real “known for their verisimilitude”
All bad novels are bad for numerous reasons; all good novels are good for their verisimilitude of reality, placing the readers in a world that resembles the one they know

veritable - truthfully, without a doubt, remember: “veracious” means truthful
Frank is a veritable life-saver -- last year, on two different occasions, he revived people using CPR.

vicissitude - change in one’s circumstances, usually for the worse
Even great rulers have their vicissitudes--massive kingdoms have diminished overnight

vitriol - abusive or venomous language used to express blame or bitter deep-seated ill will
His vitriol spewed forth from a deep-seated racism that consumed his whole life

vitriolic - abusive, harsh or corrosive in tone
While the teacher was more moderate in her criticism of the other student's papers, she was vitriolic toward Peter's paper, casting every flaw in the harshest light

vituperate - criticize harshly, synonym: berate
The drill sergeants vituperated him for petty oversights such as forgetting to double knot the laces on his boots

palpable - something that can be felt, touched, or perceived easily and clearly
The tension in the room was so palpable that you could almost reach out and touch it.

siphoning - embezzling or diverting funds illicitly from an organization or account for personal gain, may also mean “liquid” stealing, in its literal sense
The accountant was found guilty of siphoning funds from the company's accounts into his own offshore bank account.

zeitgeist - spirit of the times
Each decade has its own zeitgeist--the 1990's was a prosperous time in which the promise of the American Dream never seemed more palpable
