abjure - formally reject or give up (a belief or idea)
While the church believed that Galileo abjured and gave up on the heliocentric theory under threat of torture, he later wrote a book clearly supporting the theory

abrogate - rescind, formally revoke
The workers abrogated their right to strike for four years in exchange for better health insurance

adjudicate - ‘jud’, i.e, pass judgment or make a decision on a dispute or legal matter
The judge will abjudicate the dispute between the two parties and make a final decision on the matter.

afford - provide with an opportunity
The summit of Mt. Kilimanjaro affords a panoramic view of 3 countries

alacrity - eager willingness to do something
The first three weeks at his new job, Mark worked with such alacrity that upper management knew it would be giving him a promotion

anachronism - ‘ana’ + ‘chronism’, not up to chron, i.e, not up to current times; inappropriate for the given time period
Dressed in 15th century clothing each day, Edward was a walking anachronism

anathema - a detested person; the source of somebody's hate
Hundreds of years ago, Galileo was anathema to the church; today the church is anathema to some on the left side of the political spectrum.

anemic - lacking energy and vigor (remember, anaemic person will be anemic)
After three straight shows, the lead actress gave an anemic performance the fourth night

anodyne (1) - remember “sensodyne”; something that is soothing, comforting, or non-controversial 
Muzak, which is played in department stores, is intended to be an anodyne, but is often so cheesy (OR) After a long day of work, she sought solace in the anodyne melodies of classical music.

anodyne (2) - bland and lacking substance
The politician's speech was filled with anodyne statements that avoided addressing the pressing issues of the community (OR) Wilbur enjoyed a spicy Mexican breakfast, but Jill prefered a far more anodyne, or bland meal in the mornings

antic - ludicrously odd 
The clown's antic act was too extreme for the youngest children

aplomb - great coolness and composure under strain
Dhoni acted with aplomb during the final of the IPL

apogee - the highest point, i.e, apex or zenith
The successful launch of the spacecraft marked the apogee of human achievement in space exploration

apostate - a person against (apo) the state/religion/cause or who has abandoned on it
An apostate of the Republican Party, Sheldon has yet to become affiliated with any party

apothegm - a short, pithy instructive saying, i.e, maxim
Winston Churchill is famous for many apothegms

apotheosis - exaltation to divine status; the highest point of development, similar to apogee
As difficult as it is to imagine, the apotheosis of Mark Zuckerberg's career, many believe, is yet to come

approbatory - expressing praise or approval
Although it might not be her best work, Hunter's new novel has received generally approbatory reviews.

appropriate (1) - take something by force
The government appropriated land that was occupied by squatters

appropriate (2) - allocate
The committe appropriated the funds to its various members

appurtenant - something that is connected or associated with something else, typically in a subordinate or secondary manner
In hiking Mt. Everest, sherpas are appurtenant, helping climbers in various activities (OR) The small garden shed is appurtenant to the main house, providing additional storage space for tools and equipment

arch - to deliberately tease
The baroness was arch, making playful asides to the townspeople; yet because they couldn't pick up on her dry humor, they thought her supercilious.

arrant - complete, wholly; use: an arrant fool, or an arrant idiot (usually negative)
An arrant fool, Lawrence surprised nobody when he lost all his money in a pyramid scheme

arriviste - person who has recently gained wealth, success, or social status and is considered to be lacking in cultural refinement or social grace
The city center was aflutter with arrivistes who each tried to outdo one another with their  ostentatious sports cars and chic evening dress
